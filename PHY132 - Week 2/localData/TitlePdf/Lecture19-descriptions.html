"Brief Review - RHR & Magnetic Force"
From last lecture, Prof. Hemmick covers how to calculate the magnitude and direction of magnetic force given a magnetic eld on a
charged particle moving in the eld. He uses centripetal acceleration to calculate the radius of curvature of the particle's path.
"Magnetic Spectrometer"
Using a charged particle moving in a uniform magnetic eld, Prof. Hemmick explains what kind of useful qualities about the particle
we can learn from this magnetic spectrometer, via the radius of curvature formula we calculated before.
"Magnetic Field from Moving Charges"
Moving charged particles are important for everyday life. Prof. Hemmick does some reformulation to show that magnetic force from a
single charge is actually the same as magnetic force from a bunch of moving charges (current!).
"Wire in a Magnetic Field"
Prof. Hemmick draws a wire loop in a uniform magnetic eld, and shows what happens to the current. He shows that the net force on
the object turns out to be zero, but the net torque is not zero.
"Torque and Magnetic Moment"
We look to explain the torque shown in the last section with a formula, so Prof. Hemmick introduces magnetic moment, which is the
current that we got from the magnetic eld, times the area vector.
"Review - Consequences of Magnetic Field"
Prof. Hemmick reviews the consequences of a magnetic eld, which are magnetic force, torque, and magnetic moment.
"Review - Sources of Magnetic Field"
Prof. Hemmick reviews that the net ux of magnetic eld through a closed surface must be zero. Additionally, we review the right
hand rule for current which is a source of magnetic eld.
"Ampere's Law"
From last class, we go over the Ampere's Law formula briey explained. In this example, we follow the eld (a circular path) of length
L, and determine how much current passes through this closed circle. Additionally, he alters the example to change I_in (current
inside the loop).
