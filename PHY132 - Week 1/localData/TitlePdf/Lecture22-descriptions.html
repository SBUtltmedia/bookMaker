"What We've Covered - Faraday's Law"
Prof. Hemmick reviews what we have done so far for Faraday's Law: magnetic eld changing with time, area changing
with time, and now introduces the angle between the magnetic eld and area vectors changing with time.
"Rotational Motion for Field & Area"
We introduce the case where the angle between the magnetic eld vector and area vector changes with time, and
calculate what the change in ux and the voltage would be of such a system.
"Root-Mean-Square of the Voltage"
In order to nd the average voltage of a function which changes sinusoidally with time, Prof. Hemmick describes how to
take the root-mean-squared of the function in order to meaningfully quantify the "average" voltage.
"Anecdote about V_rms - Summary"

electricity.
"Intro to Transformers"
Prof. Hemmick introduces a setup with two coils wrapped around each other, and explains that the magnetic eld is
shared between the two coils. From this, he can create a proportionality between the input and output voltage, and the
number of coils.
